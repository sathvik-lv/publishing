02Construction — What's Different
Both books draw from the same 22-cell universe — 7 FX pairs + 15 equity-index / index-combo cells — with cells selected on a train-only robustness score and locked before any sizing or stop-loss tuning. What differs is how the cells combine at the book level:

Book 1  weights every cell equally (1 / 22). Maximum compounding under a per-cell drawdown cap.   Book 2  weights each cell by inverse realized volatility. Smoother path, higher Sharpe, higher out-of-sample Sharpe.

Per-cell sizing is identical in both books: the minimum of three independent constraints — a defensive Kelly fraction, a per-cell max-drawdown rule, and a hard book-level leverage cap. The leverage cap has never bound (actual gross leverage ≈ 0.15x). Cell list, signals, and exact parameters are held in the private research repo.

⚠ Caveats — Read These

  1. Pure compound-math outputs. Final equity numbers ($72.6B Book 1, $11.0B Book 2) are compound artifacts over 10 years on a fixed $1M base with no withdrawals. The load-bearing metrics are Sharpe, Calmar, CAGR, and max drawdown — not the absolute terminal dollar figure.
  2. QuantConnect paper / live validation in progress. A live paper-trade overlay is being set up on QuantConnect to measure real execution, real slippage, and real fill quality — so the published numbers are not only clean-math backtest outputs. Results will replace the current engine-only figures when the live window accumulates.
  3. Slippage is flat. The engine uses a fixed-cost slippage model; a calibrated impact-aware TCA curve (Almgren-Chriss style) is pending before any real sizing. Sensitivity sweeps pass up to +5 pips of added cost without degrading Sharpe.
  4. Max drawdown is daily-aggregated. The −19.7% figure is a property of the daily PnL series. Intraday peaks are larger; no intraday halt rules are modeled in the current engine.
  5. Selection methodology. Cells were Calmar-selected on a train-only window with COT masking, then frozen before any SL or K tuning — so the sharpe is not a selection artifact. Production deployment would add rolling-DD circuit breakers and a news-window blackout.
  6. Bootstrap & walk-forward pass. 5 000-replicate block-bootstrap gives P(Sharpe > 1.5) = 100%; 11 / 11 positive years on the full window. But deployment at scale still needs the live paper run above before any real capital is committed.
