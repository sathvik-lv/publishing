05Strategy Construction — High Level
Structure. Calendar spread on NSE index options: sell the near-expiry (weekly) leg, buy the same-strike far-expiry (monthly) leg. Profit captured from the differential in theta decay between the two legs. Exits are rule-based across multiple conditions (time-based, order-flow reversal, trend decay, expiry roll-off).

Universe. Four NSE index families — NIFTY, BANKNIFTY, FINNIFTY, MIDCPNIFTY — with each side (CE / PE) either active or disabled per the book's locked allocation rules. Capital deployment is capped at 60% of book equity at any time.

Sizing. Per-side allocation is a fraction of book equity, derived from a defensive Kelly-style rule on the per-combo historical edge. VIX level rebalances the CE-vs-PE capital split weekly. Exact strike rules, allocation percentages, entry filters, and exit thresholds are held in the private research repo.

⚠ Caveats — Read These

  1. Data limited — not yet fully backtested. Window is 2020-01 → 2025-12 (6 years). Out-of-sample robustness claims are not yet available; a walk-forward framework on a wider dataset is pending.
  2. Exploring better data sources. Actively looking for more accurate option-chain and tick-level datasets so the strategy can be backtested more rigorously and on a wider scale than current EOD bhavcopy-based data allows.
  3. SEBI Oct 2024 circular limits live executability. Only NIFTY weekly options remain post-change. BANKNIFTY and FINNIFTY weekly legs in this backtest would NOT be executable today — treat their contribution as historical research only.
  4. Research / simulation only. No live orders placed. Execution realism depends on accurate entry/exit prices at the option-chain level, which this backtest approximates.
  5. Synthesized spot series. FINNIFTY and MIDCPNIFTY spot are constructed from a linear regression on NIFTY + BANKNIFTY (no direct historical spot). Introduces model risk on those legs.
  6. Flat slippage. 0.2% per leg (0.8% round-trip across 4 legs) is a simple uniform cost. Real execution may differ, especially in illiquid far-expiry strikes or stressed regimes.
  7. Tax views are post-processed, not independent backtests. The three views recompute equity using the same pre-tax annual P&L stream; they do not re-run the strategy at different tax rates. Slippage and cost model stay identical across all three.
